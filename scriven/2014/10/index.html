Congratulations and big thanks to Mr Winer: 20 years of blogging#

Tweet This Post#

Having read a lot of what people have been posting about Dave Winer the last few days I got to thinking a fair amount of how the pioneering technologies he has been involved with have enriched my life over the years. It turns out they have had big impacts.#

I immediately think of RSS and Podcasts. If there were two technologies I literally couldn't live without in my day to day life it's these. The amount of information I have ingested over the last 10 or so years using these is basically immeasurable and what that has done to evolve my career and overall interests in IT and other subjects I can't even begin to fathom. And the idea of treating internet content as an inbox still amazes me.#

A few years ago I discovered by chance that the BBC syndicated all their podcasts (and still do) via a single OPML file. The format was easy to understand and in those days there was no easy way to discover podcasts that they published. With a HTC Magic and an Android developer account I started to learn Java and released a podcast browsing app. Had this open format not existed I have no doubt I'd never of embarked on such a journey.#

This blog post is written using Fargo, published using a self hosted publisher and due to its flexibility I'm able to self host the pages. Fargo is a piece of software that I have daily interaction with and now couldn't live without for all sorts of content generation. It's also through Fargo I started interacting with its community and the creator himself. This has lead me down all sorts of interesting avenues and I've been lucky enough to converse with other likewise souls.#

It's funny and I'm embarrassed to say it, but up until the discovery of Fargo I hadn't paid much attention to the pioneering thoughts or people behind the technologies mentioned here. I just used them, like everyone else does. But by getting more involved and taking a focussed interest in them it has really enriched my life and scratched my nerdy technical itch at the same time! #

Dave, I salute and thank you for all the work and effort you have put in over the years. I can't wait to see what comes next.#
